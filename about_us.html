About Us

LetzPwn

The first luxembourgish CTF Team

Cybersecurity is very important in the modern Internet age. As the industry grows, more and more people are needed in the field. Although cybersecurity is thought in some universities, there is often a lack of practical skills. One way to proof ones practical skills is by participating in so called Capture The Flag (CTF) competitions. These are online or on-site competitions around the globe in which teams compete by solving cybersecurity challenges.

The LetzPwn Team is currently the only luxembourgish team actively participating in CTF challenges. In other countries, CTF teams are already more widespread (Germany: 92 teams, France: 123 teams, ...) . As Luxembourg seems to lack a bit behind in this matter, we find it important to pioneer a luxembourgish community with the motivation to improve in competitions in the upcoming years.

Origin
How the adventure started
The core group consists of 10 members, most of them still studying with the exception of a couple of the members already working. This group was brought together by SMILE to compete at the ECSC 2019 in Bucharest to represent Luxembourg.

Our goals

Spread awareness about CTF’s and recruit new members

The CTF community is still very small in Luxembourg. It is important to continuously spread awareness and recruit new members to have a chance in global competitions and represent Luxembourg in the best way possible. We will try our best to find ways to contact and integrate new members: on social media, asking friends, at events, ...

Help and Train members in Cybersecurity & CTF challenges

On the discord channel, members will find resources and guides from other members on how to get started. They’ll be able to ask questions to the other members if they’re stuck or need some guidance on how to proceed. The aim is to participate in online CTF’s as much as possible (currently doing about 2 to 3 per month on weekends) so that the newbies can try to apply the knowledge they’ve acquired in a safe and judgement-free environment with the bonus of moving the team forward if they manage to solve something. During CTF’s people work together and the discord server is already very active when trying to solve challenges.

Participate in CTF’s

The main purpose of the community is to participate in CTF’s. Our end goal is to become better over the years and being able to represent Luxembourg as a real competitor in bigger CTF events.

Meetups and Workshops

Additionally to online CTF’s, we’d like to organize meetups throughout the year to compete in CTF’s while working together in a physical space. Furthermore, depending on which resources we have at our disposition, we’d love to organize workshops with experts to further develop our knowledge.

ECSC 2020

The group is supposed to be open to any age (current members ages: 15-28), but we plan to work together with SMILE to pick a few of our players to compete at next years ECSC. In concordance with that, one of our aims will be to train next years ECSC team by organizing the team selection events (with the final event to pick the team possibly being the Game of code in March) and then organize workshops/tutoring sessions with experts from SMILE.
